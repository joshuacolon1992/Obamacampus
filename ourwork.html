For the Campus

The Michael Reese Hospital site in the Mid South Side of Chicago fully represents President Barack Obama's background, accomplishments, and the values expressed during his presidency.

It's near downtown Chicago, the McCormick Place Convention Center, and Lake Michigan's magnificent shoreline. Ample transportation options already exist including public transit with stations nearby and easy access to major roadways. The site is currently served with robust technology infrastructure and is included in the City's plans for further investment.

The campus is large enough to allow for complementary enhancements beyond the Library and Museum, including parks with direct linkages to the Lake, specialized educational, and cultural opportunities, and a Civic Innovation Center (CIC), focusing especially on the sciences and health (a particular interest of the Obama family). The CIC allows for a wide range of resources: a business incubation lab offering programming for entrepreneurs; community college/workforce offerings; and new K-12 schools related to both community needs and the special opportunities provided by the OPL.

For the City

The OPL will be an enormous economic engine for the South Side and for the City as a whole, enhancing Chicago's reputation as a global city and a prime destination for tourism and new business.

A study by Anderson Economic Group, a respected economics research firm, commissioned by the University of Chicago to analyze the economic impact of a Barack Obama Presidential Library on Chicago's South Side, has concluded that the library "can provide a significant economic boon to the neighborhood and the city."
