Our Community

Bronzeville, the destination for much of the Black Migration, represents history, a current group of resourceful leaders, and opportunity. As demographic and economic conditions changed over the past half century, its population and assets have shrunk, leaving the neighborhood ripe for new development without displacement.

The Michael Reese site was cleared as the home for the 2016 Olympic village having been chosen for many of the same reasons that make it ideal for the Obama Presidential Library (OPL). Today, Bronzeville leaders are united in seeking the revitalization that the OPL can bring in the form of employment in construction, programming, and tourism; in Tourism educational and cultural enrichment; and in new residential and business opportunities.
